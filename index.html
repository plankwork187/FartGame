📊 Character Stats Guide

All stats are rated 1–5. Higher stats always make the game harder (except where noted). Lower stats = easier run.

💨 Smell (1–5)
How pungent your releases are.
Level 1 — barely a whiff; clouds are small and fade fast.
Level 5 — eye-wateringly strong; huge clouds that linger and tag NPCs from far away.
Higher = harder (bigger detection radius).
⏱ Linger (1–5)
How long smell clouds hang in the air.
Level 1 — cloud vanishes in seconds; almost no trailing risk.
Level 5 — clouds persist for a very long time, tagging NPCs who walk through long after release.
Higher = harder (longer exposure window).
💥 Accident (1–5)
How likely you are to fart accidentally.
Level 1 — near-perfect control; involuntary releases almost never happen.
Level 5 — accident-prone; involuntary farts fire often and release extra gas.
Higher = harder (more random accidents).
⚡ Frequency (1–5)
How fast gas builds up passively.
Level 1 — slow build; plenty of time between releases.
Level 5 — gas fills up very fast; you must release constantly or risk accidents.
Higher = harder (constant pressure).
🔊 Volume (1–5)
How loud your releases are.
Level 1 — whisper-quiet; sound barely travels.
Level 5 — very loud and wet; nearby NPCs react instantly even without smell.
Higher = harder (wider sound detection radius).

How to Play

↔️Hold left or right to lean and release gas. Longer hold = more powerful (and riskier) release.
👃Smell creates a lingering cloud — bigger releases make bigger, longer-lasting clouds that can tag NPCs who walk through later.
👂Loudness is instant — anyone within hearing range reacts immediately, even from a distance, with no visible cloud.
🧍NPCs exist at three depths: foreground, midground, and background. Closer NPCs notice you more easily.
📈Reach the level's gas target before Suspicion maxes out to clear the level.
👥Some levels include a companion standing right next to you with their own special twist.
⚡Each character has a Special Move — unlocked in a future update. Watch this space!
